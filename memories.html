Memories

A collection of memories made at Bebe's Bungalow

"My favorite part of Bebe’s Bungalow adventures is when Uncle Bud took my children, (Marty, Johnny, and then later Julie) to Trout Lake for a camping/fishing trip. Other friends of Uncle Bud's joined in on the exciting trip to Trout Lake. Here is a photo of the many times everyone sat around the kitchen table of Bebe’s Bungalow to celebrate the happy memories of the trip to Trout Lake. Uncle Bud and his friends used to kid Johnny that they were going to use him as bear bait."

- Margaret Pecarina

"Somebody asked some question about some esoteric concept that I happened to know the answer. I offered the answer, and a short explanation on something completely trivial and useless. After my soliloquy, Bebe comments in a low, deeper voice, 'Oooh, you're smaaaart.' She said it sincerely, but with just enough emphasis that made me think, maybe I wasn't so smart. Makes me smile just thinking about it."

- David Farley

"I have to say, one of my favorites was our first Christmas together. Being the 'first' I was so worried how my children were going to behave! The first obstacle was sitting down to dinner and having to make a choice of what silverware should be used for what part of the meal! I remember Amelia sneaking a look to me with those questioning eyes... 'Mom?' It all worked out and we enjoyed laughing at ourselves on the way home."

- Ann Del Greco

"Being on the water—whether on the Evil Medieval 4th of July flotilla, jet skiing and pulling kids on tubes, fishing, kids catching and cooking crayfish, or learning to use a trolling motor and trying to avoid hitting rocks and then later catching a rock."

- Katie Levenick

"I spent many birthdays up at the Farley cabin over the years, and I was always surprised with a delicious cake and a lovely little celebration. I will always cherish those memories!"

- Anna Schmidt

"My favorite cabin memory is when mom and I went to the perfect fishing spot where I was catching big bass and she was catching big walleyes and we had a heck of an old hoot of a time."

- Thomas Farley

"The memories I have and you allowed my family to share are priceless and will always be cherished. Watching our kids play together, taking them tubing and wakeboarding, taking a boat ride along the lake and having a snack tray packed for the cruise. Stopping for the kids to jump off the rock. Cruising over in the boat to enjoy the bamboozler pizza, of course that was a must each time we were there. Enjoying the campfire at night and then coming in and catching a movie or some show episode from a tape. And of course we have the blessing of you taking us in the Boundary Waters area to fish, camp, canoe and enjoy nature no other way. You know that was never my highlight ;) but I most certainly enjoyed getting the opportunity of doing it the five star way! And of course the memories my boys have from it, they will cherish and talk about forever made it all worth it! Farley Family, thanks so much for including and blessing us with your wonderful hospitality and love!"

- Kim Gerads

"The Cabin was our 'happy place!' When up at the cabin you were able to forget all worries in your life and just focus on relaxing and having fun. When our mom died, Katie, Mark and I decided that instead of canceling our trip to the cabin it was actually the best place for us to go. The cabin and Farley family allowed us to put aside our grief for a few days and just relax and enjoy life. I think that speaks mountains for the 'magic of the cabin.' I loved that two of my kids learned to drive a jet ski up there. I loved all of our bags games where we always made silly rules. One of our best rules was that you had to hold something in your hand at all times while playing—this was actually a way for us to not have to put our drinks down while playing. When David played with us, Cathy told him that he would have to hold the bottle of ketchup in his hand while playing. He objected to this stupid rule but he played anyway. David was very tolerant of Katie and I; it takes a special man to put up with us. This picture is when we took a boat ride and put one foot in Canada and one in the US—the kids still talk about that. Hope to be up there again soon!"

- Ann Hickey

"It’s impossible to pick a favorite memory, but one that stands out to me happened just a few years ago, when Sam, myself, and some of our other best friends got to witness the northern lights. I don’t remember exactly how we first noticed them, but once we did we were all awestruck. Most of us had never seen them before, and it was so much more incredible than I could have expected. We all went down to the dock and sat watching the lights dance around the sky. It was almost eerie how quickly the light seemed to move, and how it felt both very close to us and impossibly far away at the same time. Luckily Sam happens to have a talent for photography and she was able to take some beautiful pictures to capture the event. Seeing the lights for the first time alongside my best friends is an incredibly special memory I will never forget. It has been amazing to watch this place transform from a cozy little cabin to the stunning home that stands here today. I am so grateful to the Farley family for always welcoming me and giving me such beautiful memories, and I look forward to 'Up North' adventures yet to come!"

- Jess Nation

"One of my favorite cabin memories was getting foolishly caught in the rain while canoeing in the Boundary Waters at age 11 with my two best friends and one dramatically yelling ‘we can get there if we paddle fast enough!’ when all hope of survival seemed lost (we were only 100 yards away from our campsite and it was just rain). This is just one example of the countless memories that my friends and I all still laugh about and cherish to this day."

- Sam Farley

"My second favorite part of Bebe’s Bungalow adventures is when Uncle Bud or Max Pauchnik would use this old fireplace in the backyard of Bebe’s Bungalow to filet fish caught while fishing on Lake Vermilion. Marty and Johnny learned to filet their own fish on this old fireplace converted to a filet fishing table. I also have fond memories of the bunkhouse (which was in the backyard) where Marty, Johnny, and Julie would sleep during the many summers we visited from Texas."

- Margaret Pecarina

"I have to say I really enjoyed spear fishing for Northern Pike. A completely new activity but so many things on the up side: 1) setting up the spearing hut is pretty straightforward, 2) actually seeing fish swim by is exciting, 3) seeing big fish poke at the bait was intense, 4) spearing the dang pike was fun, and 5) I knew mom, Jerome, and the ghosts of Bebe and Max would appreciate one less pike in the lake!"

- David Farley

"I have fun memories of the Trencheff cabin—all of our kids playing there at the 'beach,' finding crayfish, using the paddle boat and Cathy being mad at the older kids who were jumping off the bridge! And how about the DNR guy stopping Grandpa Bud's pontoon boat when we had like 13 people on it and water was almost up to the floor!"

- Steve Farley

"My most favorite memory of the cabin is when our two families were playing The Game of Things one Christmas when David and my dad said something about men combing other men's hair, holding doors open for each other, and a certain vending machine idea that shall not be discussed further."

- Amelia Del Greco

"There are so many memories it's gonna be hard to pick a favorite between Ben, Nathan and myself flipping our canoe and Nathan hooking himself [in the finger with a lure] in the Boundary Waters or the late night Pokémon Monopoly championship or the classic paddle boat adventures or even jet ski madness!"

- Don O'Gorman

"My favorite cabin memory was traveling down to the dock or boathouse in the morning and going back every hour or so to search for a colossal crawfish only to release it then catch it again later that day."

- Damian Gerads

"One of my favorites has to be when we made the slip and slide off the end of the dock. Painting the [old] garage was a good one too."

- Peter Beiswanger

"Watching the sunset over Lake Vermilion while traveling backward in the paddleboat. All this of course was done with our life jackets on!"

- Katie Levenick

"Both families, Matty-tatty and Grandpa Bud decided to take the Pontoon Boat out for the afternoon. We had fun... until we were stopped by the DNR because our boat was 'over the legal load of people.' Cathy was ready with her answer—we had PFDs for everyone and there was NO list on the boat anywhere that said what the weight or person limit was on the boat! The DNR officer conceded the fact, warned Grandpa Bud that the tag was expired (he had the updated one in his truck!) and gave each of the kids coupons for ice cream cones because they had their lifejackets! Later, we went to Wolf Bay for dinner (though Aunt Lisa thought David was Steve and stuck her hand in his back pocket... oops!) then headed back to the cabin, mostly in the dark, taking on water with the full load of people! We made it back safely after a 2+ hour ride back, but decided that we likely didn't need to do that again with a 25 horse motor."

- Lisa Farley

"I had heard about Bebe's Cabin not long after Dave and Cathy first got married. A small rustic place on a lake somewhere up in the boondocks. Steve was the outdoorsman, not Dave or I. So Dave and a cabin didn't seem like a perfect match. I also had heard that they had 'fixed it up.' One presumes that they had made it more fun and livable for their kids to enjoy some hiking or fishing. So you can imagine my surprise when I finally made it to the 'cabin' and found the home of my dreams sitting on the best part of the lake! I could see why everyone wanted to 'rough it' up at the cabin... and given Cathy's penchant for keeping the kitchen stocked to the brim with great food, it's the kind of place you don't really want to leave."

- Mike Farley

"I had the amazing opportunity to spend every one of my summers during grade school (and two during college) at the cabin with my mom. There, she taught me so much—how to cook (Bebe's pasta sauce recipe, potica), drive a manual, shoot/clean a gun, drive a boat, filet a fish, and, of course, make cocktails—and, less overtly, how to be a good host, neighbor, and friend, how to learn from our mistakes, be resilient, prioritize people over all things, choose your battles, revere our elders, and be open to new ideas. Above all, she taught me how to enjoy life and live each day like it is your last. For these and so many other reasons, I am forever grateful to my mom and Bebe's cabin."

- Sam Farley

"Catherine trying to turn me into a quilter. Our sewing days together when we would talk about everything. The stories that we shared from when we were kids. The boat ride that to this day I will never let Catherine forget! The best and probably most important memory of Bebe's Bungalow, however, is the fact that after years of not getting to know my cousin Catherine, I ended up meeting a wonderful friend, who has a wonderful family. The Del Grecos are so much richer getting to know our cousins, the Farleys. I not only got to know my cousin, I ended up with something I never thought I would know... the comradery and love of a sister."

- Ann Del Greco

"Mine is going on my first real camping trip to the Boundary Waters. There were a lot of exciting memories from experiencing portaging from lake to lake, setting up camp, cooking food over the fire, swimming in the lake, seeing loons, picking blueberries and lots of fishing. However, the day that we spent fishing in Little Trout was the most memorable. Sam, Thomas and I were in a canoe together. Thomas was giving me a hard time for casting in the middle of the bay. I caught my largest fish ever, Hank—a 35-inch northern!"

- Katie Farley

"Another favorite part of Bebe’s Bungalow is this photo of Marty getting ready for a boat ride posing by the patio doors off the kitchen. The other two, (Johnny and Julie) were getting ready to go, too. Marty, Johnny, and Julie had a great time visiting Grandma Bebe during those many summers we visited. Grandma Bebe always used to say that it wasn't her they so much wanted to visit as the draw was Lake Vermilion and all it had to offer."

- Margaret Pecarina

"My favorite cabin memory was the night my friends and I were going in and out from the neighbor's sauna to the lake and back again. After it got dark, the northern lights came out and it was prime. We went inside and had several helpings of pot pie to finish the night."

- Ben Farley

"Bebe looking through some old photo albums with Cathy and they giggled looking at an image of Bebe standing in the lake with water up to mid shins... Cathy says, 'ooh Gramma, look at your long stately legs!' From then on, anytime Gramma’s legs were mentioned, they were always referred to as 'long, stately legs'... they may have been 24" at best!"

- David Farley

"A constant highlight of my trips to Bebe's Bungalow is the many card games and board games that we play together—whether it be perfecting my poker face while playing Liar's Dice, learning to play cribbage with the pros, or playing a heated game of Old Maid that lasts for hours, we always have a wonderful time."

- Anna Schmidt

"My favorite memory was when we were getting the boaters' license and we were doing all of the online training and what not, and Cathy then mentioned there would be a field test and I was so scared that I was going to fail [this super difficult, official test]... the day came and we went down to take the dreaded 'field test' and I then realized that Cathy was the field test grader! Also nothing beats hotdogs on the fire at any and all times of the day, and of course canoe wars in the Boundary Waters!"

- Tori Gosse-Gram

The best was getting up early to go find some fish for one of our last meals in the wilderness. I caught my second biggest walleye to date up there and won’t forget it (especially since we wrote the coordinates of the fishing spot on the canoe paddle)."

- Kyle Gerads

That evening, Cathy introduced me to a Key Lime Pie Cocktail which I loved. It has become my favorite drink and I frequently find myself looking up the instructions in the Farley Family recipe book, although I almost have it memorized by now."

"A funny memory I have is of waterskiing—getting pulled by Cathy on the jet ski. The babysitter guy, Matty, was a big guy and no matter how hard he tried, he just couldn’t get his skis up on the water! The water was shooting over his face and forehead as he held on for dear life!"

- Steve Farley
