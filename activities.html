Spring

Up north, the winter season can stretch all the way into April or even May, so spring is often times cut short. However, there is still plenty to do when the snow starts to melt. One of the main attractions of the season is watching "water skipping," in which snowmobilers will ride their sleds across a stretch of open water. There is a spot in particular on Lake Vermilion where people convene for this sport; however, state regulations discouraged this event, so it is now enjoyed less publicly.

Fish opener occurs during the second week of May in St. Louis County and is perhaps even more popular than water skipping. For many people, late spring is the time to get the dock and boat back in the water and catch fish in peace before the summer rush starts and the non-locals flood the lake.

With the melting of the snow also comes cleared walking paths and more active wildlife. Both people and animals can be seen more outdoors, and it is not uncommon to see wolf tracks or bear scat in the woods. Hikes, boat rides, and gardening once the ground thaws are other springtime activities enjoyed up north.
