History

The History of Township 62 North of the Range 16 West of the Fourth Principal Meridian.

“Lot 7, Section 4, Township 62, Range 16”

Dec. 27, 1898

George G. Bush purchases the land for $400

1900

Mary J. Crosby obtains property ownership, pays off $366 mortgage

1904

Mary J. Crosby records a Timber Deed by Nels Johnson for $250

1905

Mary J. Crosby grants William Washburn (who then becomes The W.D. Washburn Farm Lands Company Incorporated) ½ mineral rights for 20 years starting Jan. 1, 1906

June 1, 1906

W.D. Washburn, Jr. Farm Lands Company sells their rights for $34,320.95 to Henry L. Little and Henry P. Watson (mortgage of 6% due in 5 years)

1915

Henry and Erba Little sell ½ their interest to Jessica P. Briggs; mortgage is foreclosed, interest and property goes up for sale

March 20, 1916

Court orders W.D. Washburn Jr. Farm Company to sell property in its entirety to Henry P. Watson and Jessica P. Briggs for $39,892.96; this includes entire property, interest and cost of sale

1938

Mary E. Crosby and John Q.A. Crosby sell property to John and Ellen B. Shosie

Oct. 9, 1943

Ellen B. Shosie & John sell to Oscar & Dorothy Anderson

Nov. 1951

Oscar & Dorothy Anderson sell back to John & Ellen B. Shosie, who then sell it to Arthur & Elfreda Jacobson

Sept. 17, 1975

Arthur & Elfreda Jacobson sell to Florence Belay

Dec. 31, 1985

Florence Pauchnik & Max sell to Fred Belay & Margaret Pecarina

Sept. 7, 2001

Greenwood Township Recreational Trail Easement is signed by Florence Belay, Fred Belay, Eileen Emberson, Margaret Pecarina, John Pecarina

Aug. 11, 2005

Fred Belay and Margaret Pecarina sell to Catherine & David Farley

2007

Boathouse is remodeled and repaired

2010

New garage is built on back of property

2014

Cabin is torn down and rebuilt
